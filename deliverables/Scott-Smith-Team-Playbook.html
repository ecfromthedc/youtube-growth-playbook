Section 01
What this actually is.

Faceless long-form YouTube. No on-camera presence. Voiceover plus B-roll editing. Targeted at high-RPM niches — topics where advertisers pay $7–$12+ per 1,000 views, like aviation, finance, legal, medical, and tech-adjacent verticals. The system is modular, AI-augmented, and designed to scale across many channels in parallel.

The format

Long-form only. 10–15 minutes.

Shorts do not pay meaningfully. Long-form videos compound watch hours, attract more ad slots per view, and unlock real revenue.

The labor split

AI handles ~80%. Humans handle the rest.

Claude and Gemini handle data analysis, topic research, Script Bible generation, outlines, and analytics. A human writes the final script. A small team produces the video.

The economics

$75 in, $1,000+ potential out.

Per-video production cost: ~$75 (writer + VO + editor + thumbnail). One winner can return $1K+ over its lifetime. The portfolio carries the math.

The portfolio principle

3 of 22 videos drive 90% of revenue.

Most videos flop. Plan for it. The winners pay for everything. Same as venture capital, same as stock portfolios.

Section 03 · STEP 1 of 5
Niche Selection

Step 01
Pick a niche where the math works on Day 1.
Goal: Land in a niche that pays $7+ RPM, has rising or evergreen demand, and has a real gap in the competitive set.

The three non-negotiables

  • High RPM — minimum $7 per 1,000 views. Top niches pay $10–$12+. Scott's aviation channel: $12.09 RPM.
  • Rising or evergreen demand — no dying fads. Use Google Trends to confirm.
  • A gap in the market — demand exists but competition is thin. Fewer than ~50 mature English channels in the space.

The RPM reality

Niche choice compounds forever. A $12 RPM niche earns 6× what a $2 RPM niche earns for the exact same view count. Choose wrong, and no amount of execution fixes it.

The discovery workflow

  1. Use Claude + Gemini to brainstorm 20+ high-RPM candidate niches
  2. Validate with Google Trends — 12 months, rising or stable
  3. Check competitive density on YouTube — count mature channels, their size, their recency
  4. Shortlist niches with rising trend + thin competition
  5. Run a deep-dive validation on the top 3 (Prompt 1B in the library)
"It's like an investment portfolio: 10% of the stocks make up 90% of the value. Same thing on YouTube. You're not guessing — you're picking a niche where the math works, then running a portfolio." — Scott Smith

Section 04 · STEP 2 of 5
Video Ideation (The 4-Prompt Core Loop)

Step 02
Generate validated, data-backed video topics.
Goal: Produce a ranked list of 5 video topics with evidence — no guessing, no wasting $75 on dud videos.

The full sequence

  1. Claude — Channel Data Extraction. Screenshot the top competitor's Videos tab (40 most recent uploads). Paste into Claude. Get back a clean table: title, view count, upload date.
  2. Claude — Title Pattern Analysis. Feed Claude the table. Get back: top-performing title patterns, what's failing, topic theme clusters, hook word frequency, upload-timing signals, and the gaps a new entrant can exploit.
  3. Claude — Build the Gemini Research Prompt. Ask Claude to write a sharp, niche-specific prompt for Gemini Deep Research. Claude returns a tailored 150–300 word prompt.
  4. Gemini Deep Research. Paste Claude's prompt. Gemini researches the entire niche and returns a comprehensive doc — trending topics, recent angles, surfacing news, calendar events.
  5. Claude — Topic Ranking. Feed Gemini's research back to Claude. Get back 5 ranked topic recommendations with title variations, difficulty scores, evergreen vs. timely classification, and estimated view ceilings.

The critical verification step — Google Trends

Do not skip this. For each of the 5 Claude-ranked topics, validate manually in Google Trends. Filter: United States · Past 12 months · YouTube Search. Classify rising / stable / declining. Kill anything flat or declining unless it's truly evergreen. Compare topics side-by-side in the "Compare" feature.

Why this matters in practice: In Scott's aviation example, B21 Raider and F15E both trended up (keep). AIAA Aviation was flat — would have been $75 of production spend for $0 of return. Trends caught it before the script was even written.

"AI is a tool. It's not a replacement for your brain. Verify the data. Verify the research. The people who let AI do everything end up with channels that make no money." — Scott Smith

Section 05 · STEP 3 of 5
The Script Pack

Step 03
Build the Script Bible, generate outlines, hand to a human writer.
Goal: Deliver a research-packed, voiceover-ready final script — without writing it yourself.

3a. Build the Script Bible

  1. On the competitor channel, open 10 top-performing videos
  2. For each: scroll down → Show Transcript → Copy Transcript (or pull at scale with vidIQ)
  3. Paste all 10 transcripts into a single document, clearly delimited with title + view count
  4. Send to Claude with the Script Bible prompt (see Prompt 3-1)
  5. Claude returns the Script Bible — the template of how this niche's winning scripts actually work: hook patterns, narrative arc, research integration, tonal register, recurring phrases, tension/payoff patterns, CTA placement, retention engineering

3b. Generate the outline (NOT the full script)

  1. Pick 2–3 validated titles from Step 2
  2. Send to Gemini: Script Bible + Title with the outline prompt (Prompt 3-2)
  3. Gemini returns a detailed outline per title — hook, section beats, research to weave in, transitions, CTA placement, runtime estimate, writer notes

3c. Hand off to a human writer

Package: Script Bible + Outline + Gemini research notes = The Script Pack. The human writer takes the Pack and produces the final script.

"If you have Claude or Gemini write the full script, you're wasting your time. Quality is garbage. Use AI for the bible and the outline. Use a human for the final." — Scott Smith

Writer cost: $25–$75 per script depending on tier. Hire 3–4 writers initially for redundancy. Output: 10–15 minute video script (≈ 1,500–2,200 words at voiceover pace).

3d. Final delivery package

The script goes to three people:

  • Voiceover artist — records the audio (or use ElevenLabs AI VO)
  • Video editor — cuts the final video
  • Thumbnail designer — produces 2–3 thumbnail variants
"You didn't write a word, you didn't edit a frame. You got the video idea, you got the script pack, you handed it to the team, they handed you back a video." — Scott Smith

Section 06 · STEP 4 of 5
Getting Your First Views

Step 04
Get a video past 1,000 views. That's the signal you're "in the game."
Goal: Two things simultaneously — collect data on what's working, and hit the algorithmic threshold that unlocks broader distribution.

The two metrics that actually matter

MetricTargetWhat it means
CTR (Click-Through Rate)10%+Of people who see your thumbnail, 10% click
APV (Average Percentage Viewed)30%+Viewers watch at least 30% of the video

The reality you have to accept

  • Most early videos flop. That's normal. The algorithm is testing you.
  • 80% of your videos will get under 1,000 views. Plan for it. Don't panic.
  • 3 of every 22 videos will drive 90% of the revenue. The portfolio principle.
  • The snowball effect is real. When ONE video pops, YouTube sends ALL your videos to more people. One hit lifts the whole channel.

Publishing cadence

Minimum 2 videos per week. Below that, the algorithm doesn't have enough data to learn what you're about.

AI as your optimization partner (after upload)

Use Claude to:

  • Generate title variations for A/B mental testing
  • Analyze your hooks ("paste the script — why would someone click away in the first 30 seconds?")
  • Interpret your analytics (paste screenshots from YT Analytics → ask for takeaways)
  • SEO-optimize descriptions and tags
"If it's not working, something YOU'RE doing is wrong. Check your data. Your analytics tell you the answer. Don't abandon the niche — abandon the assumption." — Scott Smith

Section 07 · STEP 5 of 5
Monetize & Scale

Step 05
Get paid. Then duplicate the system on channel 2, 3, 4…
Goal: Hit YouTube Partner Program (YPP) thresholds → apply → start earning ad revenue → scale into a portfolio.

YPP requirements

  • 1,000 subscribers
  • 4,000 watch hours in the past 12 months (~35–40K views at 10–15 min length)

Hit both → apply → wait for review → monetized.

Pre-monetization revenue (Scott's pro tip)

You can earn before YPP via:

  • Brand deals — sponsorships baked into the video
  • Affiliate marketing — product links in the description

This matters: you can generate cash flow before the algorithm starts paying you.

Revenue maximization levers

  1. High RPM niche — locked in at Step 1, non-negotiable
  2. High APV — longer watch = more ads shown per view = higher revenue per view
  3. Long-form only — Shorts do not pay meaningfully. 10–15 min is the sweet spot.
  4. Multiple channels — the scale play

The tags hack most people ignore

Two surfaces in YouTube Studio, both undervalued:

  • Video tags (inside YouTube Studio → scroll down) — help YouTube classify your content topically and surface it in search. Example for celebrity niche: celebrity news, Hollywood drama, pop culture.
  • Description hashtags (3–5 at the top of your description) — make videos discoverable via clickable hashtag links. Example: #celebrities #HollywoodDrama #PopCulture.

The vidIQ hack: Install the vidIQ Chrome extension. When viewing any competitor's video, the right sidebar shows the exact tags they're using. Copy and adapt for your videos in the same niche.

Scaling the portfolio

Channel 1 → prove the system → hit $30K+ potential. Channel 2 → easier, same team, same process, no learning curve. Channel 3, 4, 5… → compounding income streams using the same SOPs, prompt library, and analytics dashboard. Only the niche and channel identity change.

Step 3 — Script Creation

Prompt 3-1 — Script Bible Generation Claude
Below are 10 full transcripts from top-performing videos in the [NICHE NAME] niche, sourced from the competitor channel [CHANNEL NAME]. Each video's view count is listed with its transcript.

Do a deep, structural analysis and produce a Script Bible — the template of how this niche's winning videos actually work. Cover:

1. Hook structure — the first 30 seconds across all 10 videos. What's the pattern? (Pattern-interrupt, question, bold claim, time-sensitive urgency, etc.)

2. Narrative arc — how does the script progress from hook → body → CTA? Break into typical sections with timing estimates.

3. Research integration — how is data, facts, quotes woven in? What % of script is narration vs. evidence?

4. Tonal register — sentence length, reading level, emotional intensity, authority level. Match to audience.

5. Recurring phrases and transitions — the exact verbal glue this channel uses between sections.

6. Tension + payoff patterns — how does the script keep the viewer from clicking away? Look for open loops, delayed answers, callbacks.

7. CTA patterns — how do they handle "subscribe," "next video," or sponsor integrations? Where in the script?

8. Retention engineering — specific tactics visible in the writing that target retention (drop-offs, re-hooks, visual cues the writer is cueing).

Output the Script Bible as a reusable template that a human script writer could follow for ANY new topic in this niche.

The system is modular — each step has a clean hand-off, so ownership splits cleanly across the team without losing continuity. These are the 8 roles. One person can wear multiple hats at small scale; at scale you specialize.

Daily Work

Tools

YT Studio (Editor role), Claude Pro, Google Trends, team workspace

Section 12
Metrics & Diagnostics

Two core metrics drive every decision: CTR and APV. Together they tell you exactly what's broken. Memorize the diagnostic matrix.

CTR — Click-Through Rate

CTRVerdictWhat to do
10%+Target. You're winning.Replicate the pattern
5–10%Acceptable, room to growA/B test new thumbnails on top performers
3–5%Weak, needs iterationRework thumbnail + title
<3%BrokenStop scheduling new videos in this format until fixed

APV — Average Percentage Viewed

APVVerdictWhat to do
30%+Target. Hook is landing.Document the Script Bible pattern
25–30%AcceptableSharpen the first 30 seconds
20–25%Script has holesRun Prompt 4-2 hook analysis; restructure
<20%Script is brokenStop the cluster; rework script approach

The diagnostic matrix

CTR HIGHAPV HIGH

Clicking + staying — winning

Nothing is broken. Make more videos in this exact pattern.

CTR HIGHAPV LOW

Clicking + bouncing — script problem

Thumbnail/title hook works, but the script can't hold them. Fix the first 30 seconds. Re-script future videos in the cluster.

CTR LOWAPV HIGH

Not clicking + staying — packaging problem

People who watch love it, but nobody's clicking. New thumbnails, new title variants. The content's fine.

CTR LOWAPV LOW

Not clicking + bouncing — wrong topic

Topic/niche mismatch. Rethink: is this topic actually right for this channel? Abandon and reallocate.

Secondary metrics to track

  • Subscriber conversion rate — subs gained ÷ views. Target: 1%+ established, 2%+ new channels.
  • Watch time per video — 15-min video × 30% APV = 4.5 min/view. Longer videos compound watch hours faster — critical for YPP.
  • RPM — $7+ minimum for the niche, $10+ for "good" niches. RPM × Views = Revenue.
  • Traffic source mix — healthy split: Browse 25–40%, Suggested 30–50%, Search 15–25%, External <10%.
"Check your data. Numbers don't lie. If you think a niche is dead, something you're doing is wrong." — Scott Smith

Section 13
Quality Gates — Pre-Upload & Post-Upload

Every video clears these checklists before publishing. The Publisher enforces the pre-upload gate. The Strategist owns the post-upload monitoring.

Pre-upload gate

Topic validation

  • Topic came from validated list (Prompt 2-4 + Google Trends)
  • Google Trends shows rising or stable in past 90 days
  • No obvious demonetization risk
  • Similar competitor videos performed above niche median

Title

  • Under 70 characters (won't get truncated)
  • Contains primary keyword for the topic
  • Has emotional hook (curiosity, urgency, stakes)
  • 3 variants generated, best one selected
  • Doesn't over-promise (clickbait kills APV)

Thumbnail

  • High contrast, readable at small size
  • Face or focal element (90%+ of top performers have one)
  • 2–3 variants produced minimum
  • Designer applied niche-winning patterns
  • No misleading imagery

Script

  • Hook grabs attention in first 15 seconds
  • Key question / stakes established by 30s mark
  • Research-backed (Gemini + Script Bible)
  • No bloat or slow sections
  • Length 10–15 min (sweet spot for monetization)
  • CTA placement strong

Production

  • VO clear and well-paced
  • B-roll matches narration (no generic stock)
  • Audio levels consistent
  • Captions cleaned up
  • End screen configured (subscribe + next video)

Upload

  • Title, description, tags, hashtags all set
  • 3–5 hashtags in top of description
  • Video tags include niche keywords (via vidIQ)
  • Publish time aligned to audience time zone (US prime 4–9 PM ET)
  • Playlist assignment correct

Post-upload monitoring

24-hour check

  • Impressions flowing (algorithm sending test traffic)
  • Initial CTR visible
  • Initial APV trend visible
  • No copyright or content flags

48-hour check

  • If CTR < 5%: run Prompt 4-1 CTR diagnostic
  • Look at retention graph for drop-off points
  • If both CTR + APV weak: flag for Strategist review

7-day check

  • Compare to channel median
  • Flag as winner / average / underperformer
  • Note takeaways for next week's planning

30-day check

  • Calculate cumulative revenue per video
  • Identify "3 of 22" winner category
  • Decision: replicate the cluster, or pivot direction

Section 14
When to double down vs. cut the fat.

The portfolio principle says 3 of 22 videos drive 90% of revenue. Your job is to identify the winners fast and pour more production into them, and to stop bleeding into clusters that aren't working.

↗ Double down signals

  • CTR consistently 8%+ across a topic cluster
  • APV consistently 35%+ on a specific format
  • One video from a cluster got 3× channel median views
  • Retention graph shows viewers binge-watching multiple videos in series

Action: Make 3–5 more videos in the same cluster/format within 2 weeks. Capture the algorithmic momentum.

↘ Cut the fat signals

  • Topic cluster averaging <5% CTR after 4+ attempts
  • Specific format consistently underperforming
  • Audience retention plateaus low even with script iterations
  • Zero subscriber conversion from a topic area

Action: Stop making content in that cluster/format. Reallocate production capacity to a winning cluster.
