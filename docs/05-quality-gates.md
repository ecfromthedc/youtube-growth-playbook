# Quality Gates & Metrics

## The Benchmarks That Actually Matter

Scott's non-negotiable metrics from the workshop. These are the gates we manage every client to.

---

## The Two Core Metrics

### 1. CTR (Click-Through Rate)
**What it measures:** Of people who see your thumbnail in search/suggested/home, what % click it.

**Benchmarks:**
- **10%+ = Target** (Scott's number)
- **5–10% = Acceptable, room to grow**
- **3–5% = Weak, needs iteration**
- **<3% = Broken, stop and fix**

**Diagnostic rule:**
- Low CTR = thumbnail + title problem
- This is usually the fastest fix (swap thumbnail, A/B new title)

### 2. APV (Average Percentage Viewed)
**What it measures:** Of people who clicked, what % of the video do they watch on average.

**Benchmarks:**
- **30%+ = Target** (Scott's number)
- **25–30% = Acceptable**
- **20–25% = Script has holes**
- **<20% = Script is broken, viewers bouncing hard**

**Diagnostic rule:**
- Low APV = script + pacing problem
- First 30 seconds matter most. If they watch past 30s, they usually finish.

### Diagnostic Matrix

| Scenario | CTR | APV | What's Broken | Fix |
|----------|-----|-----|---------------|-----|
| Clicking + staying | High | High | Nothing | Double down on this topic pattern |
| Clicking + bouncing | High | Low | **Script** | Fix hook, re-script or re-structure |
| Not clicking + staying | Low | High | **Thumbnail/title** | New thumbnail + title variations |
| Not clicking + bouncing | Low | Low | Wrong topic/niche | Rethink: is this topic wrong for this channel? |

## Secondary Metrics We Track

### Subscriber Conversion Rate
**Metric:** Subscribers gained ÷ views
**Target:** 1%+ for established channels, 2%+ for new channels
**Why it matters:** Healthy channels convert viewers to subs. If people watch but don't sub, your CTA + niche-fit is off.

### Watch Time Per Video
**Target:** Higher is better, but context-dependent
- 10-min video at 30% APV = 3 min watch time per view
- 15-min video at 30% APV = 4.5 min watch time per view
- **Longer videos with strong APV compound watch hours faster** — critical for YPP eligibility

### RPM (Revenue Per Mille)
**Target:** $7+ minimum for the niche; $10+ for "good" niches
**Why:** RPM × Views = Revenue. Niche selection locks this number in from Day 1.

### Traffic Source Mix
**Healthy distribution (established channel):**
- Browse features: 25–40%
- Suggested: 30–50%
- Search: 15–25%
- External/Other: <10%

**If search is dominant:** You're getting discovered but not recommended. Work on packaging (thumbnails + titles).
**If suggested is dominant + growing:** Algorithm is feeding you traffic. Good sign.
**If external is dominant:** You're not getting organic YT traffic. Relying on promotion.

## Quality Checklist — Pre-Upload Gate

Every video must pass all these before publishing. **Publisher's job to enforce.** If any fails, the video doesn't go up until it's fixed.

### Topic Validation
- [ ] Topic came from validated list (passed Prompt 2-4 ranking + Google Trends verification)
- [ ] Google Trends shows rising or stable in past 90 days
- [ ] No obvious demonetization risk (sensitive content, copyright, etc.)
- [ ] Similar videos from competitors have performed well (≥ median view count in niche)

### Title
- [ ] Under 70 characters (won't get truncated in search)
- [ ] Contains primary keyword for the topic
- [ ] Has emotional hook (curiosity, urgency, stakes)
- [ ] 3 variants generated (Prompt 2-4 or 4-1) and best one selected
- [ ] Doesn't clickbait (over-promise = low APV + channel death)

### Thumbnail
- [ ] High contrast, readable at small size
- [ ] Face or focal element (90% of top-performing thumbnails have one)
- [ ] 2–3 variants produced (minimum)
- [ ] Designer applied niche's winning patterns (from competitor analysis)
- [ ] No misleading imagery that doesn't match the video

### Script
- [ ] Hook grabs attention in first 15 seconds
- [ ] Key question or stakes established by 30-second mark
- [ ] Research-backed (from Gemini research + Script Bible)
- [ ] No obvious script bloat or slow sections
- [ ] Length: 10–15 minutes target (sweet spot for watch hours + monetization density)
- [ ] Strong CTA placement (subscribe, next video)

### Production
- [ ] Voiceover is clear, paced well (not too fast, not too slow)
- [ ] B-roll matches the narration (no generic stock where specific is needed)
- [ ] Audio levels consistent (music under VO, no sudden spikes)
- [ ] Captions auto-generated and cleaned up
- [ ] Video ends with end screen (subscribe + next video thumbnails)

### Upload
- [ ] Title, description, tags, hashtags all set
- [ ] 3–5 hashtags in top of description
- [ ] Video tags include niche keywords (matched to competitor tags via vidIQ)
- [ ] Publish time strategic (aligned to audience time zone — US prime time 4–9 PM ET for US audiences)
- [ ] End screen configured
- [ ] Playlist assignment correct

## Quality Checklist — Post-Upload Monitoring

### 24-Hour Check
- [ ] Impressions started flowing (YouTube sends initial test traffic)
- [ ] CTR visible (may be low initially as algorithm finds audience)
- [ ] Initial APV trend visible
- [ ] No flagged copyright or content issues

### 48-Hour Check
- [ ] Run Prompt 4-1 (CTR diagnostic) if CTR < 5%
- [ ] Look at retention graph for drop-off points
- [ ] If both CTR and APV are weak: flag for Strategist review

### 7-Day Check
- [ ] Compare to channel median
- [ ] Flag as winner / average / underperformer in tracking doc
- [ ] Note takeaways for next week's content planning

### 30-Day Check
- [ ] Calculate cumulative revenue per video (if monetized)
- [ ] Identify if this is in the "3 of 22" winner category or not
- [ ] Decision: do we make more videos like this? Or pivot topic direction?

## When To Double Down vs. Cut The Fat

### Double Down Signals
- CTR consistently 8%+ across a topic cluster
- APV consistently 35%+ on a specific format (explainer, ranking, deep-dive, etc.)
- One video from a cluster got 3× median views
- Audience retention graph shows viewers sticking for multiple videos in a series

**Action:** Make 3–5 more videos in the same cluster/format within 2 weeks. Capture the algorithmic momentum.

### Cut The Fat Signals
- Topic cluster averaging <5% CTR after 4+ attempts
- Specific format consistently underperforming (e.g., "react to" style videos flopping)
- Audience retention plateaus at low levels even with script iterations
- Zero subscriber conversion from a topic area

**Action:** Stop making content in that cluster/format. Reallocate production capacity.

## When To Escalate

The Strategist decides when to escalate to founder or to client. Escalation triggers:

### To Client (monthly or as needed)
- When we've discovered a niche pivot opportunity
- When an experimental format has paid off
- Any content policy strike or demonetization event
- Milestone achievement (first 1K views, monetization threshold hit, first $1K earned, etc.)

### To Founder (internally)
- Client wants to cancel
- Team capacity maxing out
- New channel opportunity worth pitching
- SOP gap that's costing hours per week
- Tool cost exceeding projections

**Section 1: What We Shipped**

- Video 2: [Title] — published [DATE] — [URL]

**Section 3: Video Performance**
- Per video: Views | CTR | APV | Watch hours | Notes

**Section 4: What's Working**
- Specific pattern or topic that's showing strength

**Section 5: What We're Changing Next Week**
- Specific tactical changes in response to data

**Section 6: Coming Up**
- Next week's topics (previewed)

---

### Monthly Strategic Report Template

Longer, narrative, sent end-of-month.

**Sections:**
1. **Month at a glance** — key numbers, won/lost, vibe check
2. **Channel trajectory** — is this channel on track toward stated goals?
3. **Best-performing video deep-dive** — what made it win, how we're replicating
4. **Niche evolution** — any shifts in the niche landscape we noticed
5. **Next month focus** — top 3 strategic bets
6. **Open questions for client** — anything we need their input on

## Team Quality Gates

### Weekly Internal QA Review (Friday)

Every Friday, the Strategist reviews:
- All work shipped this week per client
- Did every video clear the pre-upload checklist?
- Are weekly reports being delivered on time and at your team quality bar?
- Is Slack client comm timely and professional?
- Any process breakdowns worth documenting as SOP updates?

### Monthly Cross-Client Audit

Once a month, Strategist compares across clients:
- Which client is over/underperforming relative to peers?
- Are there patterns in what's working/failing that we can systematize?
- Any team member consistently producing higher-quality work we should promote or feature?

### Quarterly Strategy Audit

Every quarter, Founder + Strategist do a bigger review:
- Overall YouTube arm health: revenue, margin, retention
- Team capacity + hiring needs
- Tool stack updates needed
- Prompt library refinements
- Market trends (YouTube algorithm changes, AI tool evolution)
