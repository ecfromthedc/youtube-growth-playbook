# YouTube Growth Team Playbook

## Team Roles & Ownership

The beauty of Scott's system is that it's **modular** — each step has a clean hand-off, so we can split ownership across multiple people without losing continuity.

---

## Role 1: YouTube Strategist (Account Lead)
**Who:** 1 per 3–5 clients. Initially founder or a senior operator; eventually dedicated hire.

**Owns:**
- Client relationship + communication
- Niche strategy + channel positioning
- Weekly reporting + monthly strategy call
- Go/no-go on topic lists
- Escalation point when metrics underperform

**Daily Work:**
- 15 min AM: check each client's channel analytics in YT Studio
- 30 min: review the day's research/script output from the team, approve or send back
- Respond to client questions
- Weekly: prep strategy update

**Tools:** YT Studio (Editor role), Claude Pro, Google Trends, your team Notion workspace

**Capacity benchmark:** 3–5 clients per strategist at steady state. Less during onboarding-heavy periods.

---

## Role 2: Research & Ideation Specialist
**Who:** Mid-level content strategist. Can be existing your team team member cross-trained.

**Owns:**
- Running the 4-prompt Claude sequence (Step 2 of methodology)
- Running Gemini deep research prompts
- Google Trends verification
- Delivering ranked topic lists with evidence to the Strategist

**Weekly Deliverable:**
For each client channel, a **Topic Brief** doc containing:
- 10 validated topics (from 20+ candidates)
- Google Trends screenshot for each
- Competitor videos that performed on similar topics (with view counts)
- Recommended title variations per topic
- Strategist's ranked top 5

**Capacity benchmark:** 6–10 clients per researcher (research is largely AI-powered; human bottleneck is the verification + curation).

**Training Required:**
- Master the prompt library (Doc 05)
- Gemini Deep Research fluency
- Google Trends interpretation
- Reading YouTube Studio analytics

---

## Role 3: Script Producer (Script Bible + Outline Generation)
**Who:** Content operator who runs the AI side of scripting. Could be same person as Researcher at smaller scale.

**Owns:**
- Pulling the 10 top competitor transcripts (or using vidIQ)
- Running Claude to build Script Bible
- Running Gemini to generate the outline from Bible + title
- Packaging the "Script Pack" for the human writer
- QA of incoming scripts against the Bible

**Weekly Deliverable:**
- 2 fully-loaded Script Packs per client per week (assuming 2 videos/week)

**Capacity benchmark:** 4–6 clients.

---

## Role 4: Script Writer (HUMAN — Final Script Author)
**Who:** Contracted or in-house writer. Scott's system specifically says DO NOT have Claude/Gemini write the final. A human has to.

**Owns:**
- Taking the Script Pack and writing the final research-packed script
- Hitting the hook in the first 30 seconds
- Weaving in the research Gemini surfaced
- Following the Script Bible's tone/structure

**Deliverable:** Final script, 10–15 min when read at voiceover pace (~1,500–2,200 words).

**Cost:** Scott's number is roughly $25–$40 per script (part of the $75/video total). We may pay more for quality — budget **$40–$75/script** to start.

**Hiring:** Upwork, ProBlogger, or similar. Hire 3–4 writers to start so we have redundancy. Scott has SOPs for this in his program — we'll build our own.

---

## Role 5: Video Editor
**Who:** Contracted editor(s). Existing your team editors likely can take this on with training.

**Owns:**
- Taking the final script + research imagery + voiceover
- Producing a polished 10–15 min video
- B-roll sourcing (stock + AI-generated as needed)
- Captions, lower thirds, transitions

**Cost:** $25–$50 per video at volume.

**Hiring:** Same markets as writers. 2–3 editors for redundancy.

---

## Role 6: Voiceover Artist
**Who:** Contracted VO talent. Can also be AI VO (ElevenLabs) for some niches where audience doesn't notice.

**Owns:**
- Voicing the script
- Delivering clean audio files ready for the editor

**Cost:**
- Human VO: $0.10–$0.25/word ≈ $150–$550 per video
- AI VO (ElevenLabs): $22/mo for unlimited at quality tier

**Recommendation:** Start with ElevenLabs for speed + cost. Switch to human VO for clients who value it or niches where it's obvious.

---

## Role 7: Thumbnail Designer
**Who:** Graphic designer with YouTube thumbnail experience specifically. Different skill from general design.

**Owns:**
- 2–3 thumbnail variants per video for testing
- Following high-CTR patterns for the niche
- Rapid iteration based on CTR data

**Cost:** $15–$35 per thumbnail at volume.

**Hiring:** 1–2 designers minimum. your existing design team likely has someone who can cross-train.

---

## Role 8: Publisher / Coordinator
**Who:** Operations person. Part-time role, or junior hire.

**Owns:**
- Uploading to YouTube Studio
- Setting tags, descriptions, hashtags
- Scheduling publish times per client's time zone strategy
- Adding end screens + cards
- Filing analytics screenshots weekly

**Capacity:** Can handle 20+ client channels comfortably — this is a high-leverage low-skill role.

---

## The Weekly Operating Rhythm

### Monday — Planning Day
- **8 AM:** Researcher pulls analytics from every client channel, posts summary in Slack `#yt-analytics`
- **9 AM:** Strategist reviews, flags any underperformers
- **10 AM:** Research sprint for the week's videos (if not already done)
- **Afternoon:** Topic Briefs sent to Strategist for approval

### Tuesday — Production Day 1
- Script Producer runs Script Bible + outline for approved topics
- Script Pack sent to writers
- First video of the week goes live (scheduled prior)

### Wednesday — Write & Edit Day
- Script writers working on final scripts
- Editors cutting previous video
- Thumbnail designers producing variants

### Thursday — Publish & Measure
- Second video of the week goes live
- Publisher sets tags, descriptions, hashtags
- Analytics check at 24 hrs, Strategist notes any emerging patterns

### Friday — Iterate & Report
- Weekly client report goes out (auto-generated + human review)
- Strategist leaves notes for next week's niche + topic direction
- Team retro: what worked, what broke, what's the fix

## Training Plan For Existing your team Team

### Week 1 — Onboarding
- Day 1: Watch this workshop recording (Scott's 57-min session) with everyone on the team who will touch YouTube
- Day 2: Walkthrough of the 5 steps using a real live example on a test channel
- Day 3: Hands-on — everyone runs the 4-prompt sequence on their own sample niche

### Week 2 — Practice
- Each team member produces 1 full Script Pack on a practice channel
- Peer review
- Address gaps

### Week 3 — Live Run
- Pick 1 client (or team-owned test channel) and run the full workflow end-to-end as a team
- Founder + Senior Strategist observe and coach

### Week 4 — Specialization
- Each team member picks their primary role (researcher, script producer, etc.)
- Deep-dive training on their specialty
- Ready for real client work at start of month 2
