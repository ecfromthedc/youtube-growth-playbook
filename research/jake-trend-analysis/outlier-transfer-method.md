# Jake Trend — The Outlier Transfer Method (Brand-Led B2B YouTube)

**Companion track to the Scott Smith faceless playbook.** Scott's method optimizes for ad RPM on faceless automation channels. Jake's method optimizes for **high-ticket deal flow** on founder/brand-led channels. Same research-first discipline; different end goal.

- Sales-niche business channel grown **0 → 10K subs in ~60 days**, now ~20K from **12 videos**.
- First video (267K views, organic, no ads) closed a **$45,000 deal** with an American Express partner; buyer is a PE investor now interested in the company.

## The Core Thesis

> The video **idea** is the most important variable. YouTube is winner-take-all — the best idea in a niche absorbs all the attention. You don't invent ideas; you **find proven outliers and transfer them into a niche where nobody's done it yet.**

### The loop
1. **Research** — watch huge volumes of YouTube; find videos that over-performed **relative to channel size** (e.g. 230K views on a ~10K-sub channel = outlier).
2. **Reverse-engineer** — list *why* it worked: title format, thumbnail style, raw-vs-polished, point-count, stakes, challenge structure.
3. **Separate signal from noise** — keep the causal factors, discard the rest.
4. **Transfer** — apply the format to your niche, **without changing the execution** (the execution may have carried 40–50% of the win; changing it adds risk and can destroy the reason it worked).
5. **Validate & stack** — each "why" you confirm becomes a reusable tool. Stack them into a Swiss-army-knife for any niche you enter.

## The Transferable Format Library

| Format | Template | Origin outlier (per video) | Execution rules |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speedrun / "I show everything"** | `speedrunning [outcome] from $0 — I show everything` | "speedrunning an entire Shopify store (I show everything)" — 230K views on ~10K-sub channel | Lowercase title, raw/homemade thumbnail, full A→Z journey, achieve result on camera |
| **"I did X [huge number] and learned this"** | `I made 500,000 calls and learned this` | Ed Lawrence "I made 300 YouTube videos and learned this" — 579K views | Use **20–40 points** (not 3–5); fast-paced, high value-per-second is the differentiator |
| **"Watch me [do X] in 10 hours"** | `watch me book 10 sales calls in 10 hours` | Dea "watch me make and sell a digital product in 10 hours" outlier | Lowercase, homemade thumbnail, **proof in thumbnail**, set **stakes/a bet** so viewers root for the creator |
| **"[Famous person]'s advice on X"** | `Alex Hormozi's advice on cold calling` | Hormozi-advice format that out-performed even in niches he doesn't cover | Find a niche the person *doesn't* usually cover that went outlier, then apply to niches they *do* |

**Cross-cutting packaging signals**
- Lowercase titles (reads casual/authentic, not orchestrated).
- Homemade-looking thumbnails (anti-clickbait signal in over-polished niches).
- Proof imagery (payment screenshots, even if "slightly low-res" — reads real).
- Raw editing where the niche is saturated with hyper-optimized content = gap in the market.
- **Scripted-but-feels-raw:** script the hook word-for-word in casual language so it feels unplanned while being meticulous.

## Counter-Intuitive Takeaways

- **Retention is not the gate.** Hero video: 200K+ views at **5.7% retention.** Idea + packaging drove distribution.
- **"Subscribers from your website" ≠ YouTube growth.** Starting subs that came from off-platform promo don't count; grow *on* YouTube organically.
- **Don't change a winning structure** just to feel original — that's where most operators kill their own outlier transfer.
